The name was chosen in honour of Eugène Rambert (1830 - 1886), professor of French literature, poet, great naturalist and devoted member of the Swiss Alpine Club. The society provides financial support, assisted by the local railway group operating the rack railway from Montreux up to the Rochers-de-Naye. The garden cultivates plants of the mountainous regions worldwide and especially from Switzerland.
