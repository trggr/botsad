9-About the Real Jardín Botánico Juan Carlos I        The Juan Carlos I Royal Botanic Gardens is a Botanical Garden dedicated to research, conservation and divulgation of flora and environment and belonge to the University of Alcalá. It began in 1991 on an area of 26 hectares in the external campus of the University. The flora is represented in the following exhibition areas: World Flora, Iberian Flora, Regional Flora and Special Collections. Parallel has worked hard on bringing all spaces and collections from the beginning to optimize their didactic use. It is an experimental and educational resource for the university community.
